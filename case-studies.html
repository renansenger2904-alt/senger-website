01 · Eco-tourism & modular · WA & international

Luxury glamping & off-grid eco-resorts: concept to build-ready structure.

Sector: Eco-tourism / modular construction

The challenge

An architect's render of a luxury glamping resort looks striking, but someone has to make it stand up: transportable, durable and safe on remote sites with wind, sun and no easy access to trades. The gap is turning the vision into a structure a workshop can fabricate and a crew can erect in the bush.

What I did

Led the engineering that takes the concept to reality, PVC tensile-membrane roof systems, steel arch frames, modular floors and prefabricated assemblies built for transport, plus advanced modular foundations and structural anchoring for variable remote ground. Designed to AS 4100 and AS/NZS 1554 with wind-region classification, coordinating fabricators and on-site erection.

The result

Build-ready structural packages, drawings, assemblies and fabrication detail, for eco-tourism resorts delivered across Australia and overseas. Designs that survive the site walk: buildable, transportable and durable in genuinely challenging environments, without losing the architectural intent that sells the resort.

  • Structural design of tensile-membrane + steel arch systems
  • Modular, transportable floors, foundations and anchoring
  • Design to AS 4100 / AS/NZS 1554 with wind-region classification
  • Fabrication detail and on-site erection coordination

02 · Industrial · Heavy industry & food/beverage

Pipe racks & steel structures for heavy industry.

Sector: Industrial / metallurgical / food & beverage

The challenge

Major industrial clients, including a major international brewery, needed pipe racks, piping layouts and steel structures designed, calculated and detailed for fabrication, on tight schedules and to strict compliance, without stalling plant operations.

What I did

Delivered the full package behind the build: designed and detailed pipe racks, platforms and metallic frameworks; ran the structural calculations, load-bearing capacity, wind and equipment loads, in SOLIDWORKS and CypeCAD; produced fabrication drawings, BoMs and assembly guides; and worked to ISO 9001, NR 12 and NR 13, including QA inspection of boilers, pressure vessels and structural components.

The result

Build-ready fabrication packages and validated structural designs across multiple large-scale industrial sites, drawings the workshop could build straight from, with the engineering and compliance checks already done. The large-plant discipline now applied to WA fabricators.

  • Pipe racks, platforms and structural steel design
  • Structural calculations, load, wind and equipment cases
  • Fabrication drawings, BoMs and assembly guides
  • QA inspection to ISO 9001 / NR 12 / NR 13
Pipe racks & structural steel Structural calcs (load / wind) SOLIDWORKS + CypeCAD ISO 9001 · NR 12 · NR 13
03 · Energy / combustion · Engineering verification

Boiler thermal-efficiency analysis: diagnosing a 64.3% performer.

Sector: Energy / combustion · Engineering analysis

The challenge

A mixed wood-log-fired boiler rated 12,000 kg/h of steam at 12 bar was suspected of running inefficiently, but nobody had quantified how inefficient, or why.

What I did

Ran a full thermal-performance study using the indirect (heat-loss) method, taking field measurements of combustion flue gases and fuel consumption, then closed the energy balance with combustion and thermodynamic analysis to isolate the losses.

The result

A quantified verdict, 64.3% thermal efficiency, and the root cause: excess combustion air dropping flue-gas temperature and heat-transfer capacity, compounded by low steam output relative to fuel burned. A clear, evidence-based diagnosis pointing straight at the fix, the kind of verification work where a registered engineer's sign-off carries weight.

  • Indirect-method (heat-loss) efficiency analysis
  • Field measurement of flue gases and fuel consumption
  • Combustion and thermodynamic energy balance
  • Root-cause diagnosis and corrective recommendation
