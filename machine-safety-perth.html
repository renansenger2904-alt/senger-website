01What I assess

Safe machines, documented.

A clear, written machine safety assessment your team and a WorkSafe inspector can both follow.

  • Hazard identification on the machine or production line
  • Guarding and safeguarding assessment to AS/NZS 4024
  • Risk rating of each hazard (likelihood and severity)
  • Prioritised, practical safeguarding recommendations
  • Fixed guards, interlocks and presence-sensing options
  • Plant risk assessment documentation under the WHS Regulations
  • Compliance gap report against AS/NZS 4024
  • Clear actions your fabricator or maintenance team can build
02How we work

Built on real machine-safety experience.

I'm a mechanical engineer with ten years in heavy industry, including machine guarding and safeguarding on production lines for food, beverage and metallurgical plants. I assess your machines against AS/NZS 4024 and the WHS Regulations the same way every time: find the real hazards, rate them, and tell you exactly what to change.

You get a free scoping call, then a fixed-price assessment with the scope and turnaround agreed upfront. The deliverable is a written report: hazards, risk ratings, and prioritised safeguarding actions your team can act on straight away.

Clear on scope: I carry out the risk assessment and safeguarding recommendations as a competent person. Where a job needs statutory design verification or certified sign-off, that is coordinated with a credentialed verifier, and I tell you upfront when that applies. No overclaiming.

Typical sites: food and beverage processing, metal fabrication shops, plastics and packaging, workshops and small manufacturers. NDA on request, your site information stays confidential.
